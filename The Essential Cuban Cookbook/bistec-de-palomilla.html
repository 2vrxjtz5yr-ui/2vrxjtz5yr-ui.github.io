Bistec de Palomilla / Pan-Fried Top Sirloin Steak

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 30 minutes to marinate | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Serves 4

Here are the guidelines to success when cooking the steak for this recipe: 1. The steaks have to be cut thin. 2. Do not buy sirloin tips by mistake. 3. Onions are a must. 4. A squeeze of fresh lime juice is also a must. 5. Eat right away for maximum flavor.

Technique Tip: If your supermarket does not carry thin-cut steaks, pound thicker cuts with a meat mallet.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 (1½-pound) top sirloin steaks, cut no more than ¼ inch thick
  • 1 large sweet onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 lime, halved

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, combine 1 tablespoon of oil, the salt, garlic powder, onion powder, and pepper.
  2. Pat each steak dry and rub the oil and spice mixture evenly over the steaks. Cover and set aside for 30 minutes.
  3. In a large skillet or on a flat griddle, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Add the steaks, working in batches so as not to overcrowd the skillet. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes on one side, depending on the thickness. Flip and cook the steaks for 1 to 2 minutes on the other side. Transfer the steaks to a plate, cover with aluminum foil, and let rest.
  4. Immediately add the onion and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. To serve, place each steak on an individual plate and top with the sautéed onion and garlic, fresh parsley, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
