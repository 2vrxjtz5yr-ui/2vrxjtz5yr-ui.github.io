Pastelitos de Guayaba y Queso Crema / Guava and Cream Cheese Pastries

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 40 minutes to thaw and 10 minutes to freeze | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Makes 9 pastries

For years, I felt intimidated by the process of baking layers and layers of flaky crust, and so I only bought pastelitos at a Cuban bakery. That is, until I discovered store-bought frozen puff pastry. Game changer! Here's my easy spin on this classic bakery staple.

Worth the Wait

Variation Tip: For a vegan option, use vegan cream cheese and almond milk instead of the egg wash.

Ingredients

  • 2 sheets frozen puff pastry
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 bar guava paste, cut into ¼-inch slices
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

  1. Thaw the puff pastry at room temperature for 40 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl and using a handheld mixer, or in a stand mixer, beat the cream cheese, powdered sugar, and salt together for 2 to 3 minutes, or until well mixed, light, and fluffy.
  4. Dust a work surface with the flour. Lay the pastry sheets on the floured surface. Gently roll the sheets until slightly stretched. Using a sharp knife, cut each sheet into 9 squares. Place the pastry squares on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Lay an equal number of guava slices on each square, leaving a ¼-inch border. Spread 2 tablespoons of cream cheese mixture on top of the guava on each pastry and cover with another pastry square. Gently press down the center and around the edges. With a small sharp knife, score the tops. Transfer the baking sheet to the freezer for about 10 minutes. (When the pastries are well chilled, the butter will melt more slowly in the oven and the pastries will hold their shape better.)
  6. In a small bowl, combine the egg and water to form an egg wash and brush the top of the pastries with it.
  7. Bake for 15 minutes, or until golden brown. Serve warm.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
