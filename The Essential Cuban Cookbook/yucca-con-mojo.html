Yucca con Mojo / Yucca with Garlic Sauce

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 40 minutes | Serves 4 to 6

Yucca, also known as cassava, is a starchy root vegetable similar to a potato but with an earthier flavor. Cubans make a mojo sauce loaded with garlic and pour it over cooked yucca. This is the main side dish served at all Nochebuena, or Christmas Eve, celebrations. When it's time to indulge, Cubans really know how to do it!

Ingredients

  • 1½ pounds yucca, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 cups water
  • Juice of 2 limes, divided
  • Kosher salt
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • ¼ teaspoon black peppercorns
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan over medium heat, combine the yucca, water, one-quarter of the lime juice, and 1 tablespoon of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pan, and simmer for about 30 minutes until the yucca is fork-tender. Be careful not to overcook the yucca or it will turn into a puree. Drain and transfer the yucca in a single layer to a shallow plate. Remove the stringy woody center of each piece.
  2. To make the mojo marinade, in a mortar and pestle, combine the garlic, 1 teaspoon of salt, and the peppercorns and smash into a paste. Add the remaining lime juice, the orange juice, cumin, and oregano and stir to combine.
  3. In the same pan used to boil the yucca, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté for 5 minutes. Add the mojo marinade and heat for 3 to 4 minutes until bubbly.
  4. Pour the mojo sauce over the cooked yucca and serve.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
