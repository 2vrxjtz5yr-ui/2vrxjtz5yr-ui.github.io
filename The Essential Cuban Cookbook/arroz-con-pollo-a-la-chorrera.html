Arroz con Pollo a la Chorrera / Yellow Rice with Chicken

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Serves 6

Arroz con pollo a la chorrera is made with a chicken fricassee base, beer, chicken broth, and wine, just like the traditional arroz con pollo. The difference between the two is that a la chorrera is much creamier and soupier, similar to a risotto. This flavorful Cuban classic happens to be my son Nicky's favorite. It is a tradition in our home that, every year on his birthday, I make a big pot of this comfort food. Thank you, Nicky, for letting us indulge with you!

Ingredient Tip: Achiote is used for coloring and seasoning the rice. You can find it in most supermarkets or in any Hispanic or Caribbean market; Bijol is a good brand.

Ingredients

  • 3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 4 ounces Spanish chorizo sausage, casing removed, halved lengthwise, then cut into half-moons
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers, 1 chopped and 1 sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon achiote (annatto) powder
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 14 ounces Valencia or pearl rice, rinsed
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 6 ounces light beer
  • ½ cup frozen peas

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, season the chicken with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ¼ teaspoon of pepper.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or pan, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat. Add the chorizo and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer the chorizo to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot used to cook the chorizo, combine the seasoned chicken, onion, chopped bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté for 2 to 3 minutes. Stir in the tomato sauce, wine, bay leaf, garlic powder, achiote, oregano, remaining 1 teaspoon of salt, and remaining ¼ teaspoon of pepper and bring the broth to a boil. Once boiling, cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the rice, broth, and chorizo. Cover the pot and simmer for 15 minutes. Pour in the beer and stir gently.
  5. Add the peas and sliced bell pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes, or until the rice is tender. The liquid does not have to be fully absorbed.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
