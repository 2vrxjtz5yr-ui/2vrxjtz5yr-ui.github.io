Mojito

Serves 1 | Prep Time: 5 minutes

If a refreshing cocktail is your vibe, this one has your name all over it (and mine!). It's easy to make just one drink, but it's just as easy and super fun to quadruple the recipe and serve it in a pitcher to share with friends; just add the sparkling water right before serving. While you're at it, you might as well make some Pasta de Bocadito (page 9) to go with your mojitos.

Variation Tip: Puree 4 or 5 peeled cucumber slices and 1 cup cubed watermelon in a blender. Add to the shaker and you have a cucumber-watermelon mojito!

Ingredients

  • 10 fresh mint leaves, plus more for garnish
  • ½ lime, cut into 4 wedges
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup ice cubes
  • 1½ ounces white rum
  • 4 ounces sparkling water

Instructions

  1. In a cocktail shaker or a glass mason jar with a lid, combine the mint leaves and 1 lime wedge. Lightly muddle for about 5 seconds until aromatic (overly crushing the mint leaves can result in a bitter taste). Add 2 more lime wedges and the sugar and continue to muddle a few more times to release the juice.
  2. Add the ice to the shaker, pour in the rum, cover, and shake vigorously. Strain into a serving glass and top with the sparkling water. Garnish with the fresh mint and the remaining lime wedge.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
