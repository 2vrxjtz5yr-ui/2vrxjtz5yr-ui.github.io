Tostones / Fried Green Plantains

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | 4 servings

Plantains are like oversized bananas, and although they are a fruit, they are eaten and cooked like a vegetable. You can boil, fry, or roast them, but fried is the quintessential preparation for a side dish. You'll find plantains in every Cuban kitchen, ready to be cooked regardless of their ripeness. The green plantain is savory and perfect for mariquitas and tostones, whereas the yellow/black plantain is ripe and sweet and used for plátanos maduros.

Technique Tip: Using a paring knife, trim the plantain ends and cut a slit in the peel from tip to tip. Create a separation between the flesh and the skin and cut down the plantain to peel the skin off.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups canola oil
  • 2 green plantains, peeled and cut diagonally into 1-inch slices
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions

  1. Line a plate with paper towels and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat until hot. Place the plantains in the hot oil and cook for 3 to 4 minutes per side, turning occasionally, until light golden. Transfer to the prepared plate.
  3. Using a plantain press or the bottom of a heavy mug, smash the plantains to about ½ inch thick.
  4. Return the smashed plantains to the hot oil and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until golden brown on both sides. Return to the prepared plate. Season with salt. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
