Papas Rellenas / Stuffed Fried Potatoes

Prep Time: 30 minutes, plus 4 hours to chill | Cook Time: 55 minutes | 4 to 6 servings

Papas rellenas are said to be of Peruvian origin, but almost every Latin American country has its own version. This recipe is a crispy mashed potato ball stuffed with a typical Cuban picadillo filling, then coated and fried to golden perfection. You will rarely find a Cuban party without a version of this popular snack food.

Ingredient Tip: This version of picadillo is made with slightly less sauce to avoid leaking when shaping the balls.

Technique Tip: You can make the balls in bulk and freeze for up to 1 month after breading and before frying.

Ingredients

For the Mashed Potatoes

  • 4 russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons whole milk
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

For the Picadillo

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably 93% lean)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dry white wine
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup whole green pimiento-stuffed Spanish olives, halved
  • ¼ cup raisins
  • 1 tablespoon capers, rinsed and drained
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley

For the Papas Rellenas

  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup bread crumbs
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups oil

Instructions

To Make the Mashed Potatoes

  1. In a large saucepan filled with water, boil the potatoes over medium heat for about 20 minutes until fork-tender. Drain and transfer the potatoes to a bowl. Pour in the milk and season with the salt and pepper. Mash until smooth but not too soft. Set aside to cool.

To Make the Picadillo

  1. In a Dutch oven or large skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 20 to 30 seconds. Stir in the ground beef, bay leaf, tomato sauce, wine, cumin, oregano, salt, sugar, paprika, and pepper. Cook for 15 minutes. Add the olives, capers, and parsley. Cook for 5 minutes, then remove from the heat.

To Make the Papas Rellenas

  1. Scoop 2 tablespoons of cooled mashed potato into the palm of your hand. Place 1 tablespoon of picadillo in the center. Shape the potato around the filling, forming a 2-inch ball. Repeat with the remaining mashed potato and filling.
  2. In a shallow bowl, whisk the eggs and water to blend. In a second shallow bowl, mix the bread crumbs and flour to combine. Dip each ball in the egg wash, coat in the bread crumb mixture, then dip in the egg wash again and coat in bread crumbs again. Cover and refrigerate the potato balls for 2 to 4 hours before frying.
  3. In a Dutch oven or large skillet, heat enough oil to cover the balls halfway to 350°F. Working in batches, fry the balls for 3 to 4 minutes, or until golden brown. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
