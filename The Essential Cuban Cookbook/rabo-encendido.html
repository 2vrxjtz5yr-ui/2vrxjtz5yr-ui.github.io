Rabo Encendido / Braised Oxtail

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 3 hours 15 minutes | Serves 4

Oxtail, as its name implies, comes from the tail of cattle and it requires several hours of braising to become very tender. The major flavors of this dish come from hours of simmering in a rich red wine and tomato paste sauce with garlic and spices. Nothing is better on a chilly night than a dinner of slow-cooked braised oxtail. Serve this one-pot cozy stew over creamy mashed potatoes, white rice, or with a side of crusty bread. Soaking up the saucy goodness is the best part of this meal!

Technique Tip: Oxtail is sold in sections. For best results, cut it into 2-inch pieces. To make trimming the fat easier, freeze the oxtail for 30 minutes before prep.

Ingredients

  • 3 to 4 pounds oxtail, outer layer of fat trimmed
  • 2½ teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 6 large garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 cups full-bodied red wine (such as Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • 2 bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F.
  2. Rinse the oxtail pieces under cold water and pat dry. Season with 1½ teaspoons of salt and the pepper and lightly dust with the flour.
  3. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the oxtail to the pot and sear for 4 to 5 minutes per side. Transfer the oxtail to a plate and set aside. Drain any excess fat from the pot and return it to the heat.
  4. Add the carrots, onion, and bell pepper. Sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until tender. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Stir in the remaining 1 teaspoon of salt, the oregano, and cumin, then the tomato paste, wine, broth, bay leaves, and oxtail. Cover the pot with a lid.
  5. Braise for 3 hours, or until the meat is very tender but not falling apart. Remove the lid during the last 20 minutes of cooking to let the sauce reduce. Remove and discard the bay leaves before serving.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
