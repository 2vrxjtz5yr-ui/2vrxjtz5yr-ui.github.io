Pescado Frito Entero / Whole Fried Fish

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 15 minutes to marinate | Cook Time: 10 minutes | 4 servings

Just imagine sitting by the ocean at an open-air Cuban café, breathing in the fresh air, eating whole fresh fish with fried tostones, and drinking an ice-cold beer. With this recipe, you can easily escape to Cuba—or any Caribbean island. Best of all, this dish takes only about 30 minutes from start to finish. You can ask your fishmonger to clean and scale the fish for you, if you don't want to do it yourself.

Ingredient Tip: When buying fresh fish, look for plump, clear eyes and bright, shiny skin. When gently poked, the skin should feel firm and bounce back.

Ingredients

  • 2 small whole red snapper or yellowtail fish, cleaned and scaled
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon sweet smoked paprika
  • Canola oil, for deep frying
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Rinse the fish with cold water and pat dry. Score 3 cuts along the length of the fish on each side.
  2. In a mortar and pestle or food processor, combine the garlic and 1 teaspoon of salt. Smash or pulse to form a paste. Stir in the olive oil until combined. Rub the garlic-olive oil mixture all over the fish, including inside the cavity. Season with ½ teaspoon of salt, the oregano, and pepper. Set aside for 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, stir together the flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, paprika, and remaining 1½ teaspoons of salt. Dredge the fish in the flour mixture.
  4. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom skillet, heat enough oil to cover the fish to 350°F. You can tell when the oil is hot enough by dipping the end of a wooden spoon in it—if it starts to bubble around the tip, the oil is ready.
  5. Fry the fish for 4 to 5 minutes on one side, then carefully flip it and fry for 4 to 5 minutes on the other side until golden brown. Transfer the fish to a wire cooling rack.
  6. Squeeze lime juice over the fish and serve.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
