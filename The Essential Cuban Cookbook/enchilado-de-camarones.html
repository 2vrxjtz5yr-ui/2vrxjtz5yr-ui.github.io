Enchilado de Camarones / Shrimp Creole

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | 4 servings

When I was growing up, this Cuban version of shrimp Creole was made for all special occasions. It is not only wonderfully tasty but also quick and easy enough to prepare any day of the week, so you don't need to wait for a special occasion. This saucy shrimp dish is typically served with fluffy white rice and fried sweet plantains.

Technique Tip: If time allows, make the sauce 30 to 45 minutes in advance (without adding the shrimp) to allow the flavors to develop.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 large bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ¼ cup ketchup
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 pounds large (21 to 25 count) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and bay leaf and cook for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds, making sure the garlic does not burn. Stir in the tomato sauce, ketchup, wine, water, Worcestershire sauce, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the shrimp to the sauce. Stir well to combine and simmer for 3 to 4 minutes. Add the parsley and hot sauce (if using). Remove from the heat and set aside for 5 to 10 minutes. Serve over white rice.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
