Guiso de Maiz / Corn Stew

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | 6 servings

Let's hear it for the Cubans and all things saucy! Here's another soupy dish that goes great with Cuban bread for dipping into the sauce, or serve it as a main course with white rice. This recipe was handed down to me by my cousin Isabel, who had it handed down to her by our Tia Carlotta. This is how it goes in Cuban traditions—recipes travel from generation to generation.

Ingredients

  • 6 fresh ears corn, husked and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 12 ounces pork loin, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 12 ounces smoked ham, cut into 1½-inch cubes
  • 8 ounces Spanish chorizo sausage, casing removed, halved lengthwise, then cut into half-moons
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can whole tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • ¼ cup butternut squash, cubed
  • ¼ cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon dry sherry
  • 1 tablespoon sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Slice 2 ears of corn into 1-inch rounds and cut the kernels off the remaining 4 ears.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the pork and brown for 3 to 4 minutes. Add the ham and chorizo and cook for 3 minutes. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 4 to 5 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Stir in the tomatoes with their juices, the broth, squash, vinegar, sherry, paprika, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of pepper. Add the corn. Partially cover the pot and simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the corn and squash are fork-tender.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
