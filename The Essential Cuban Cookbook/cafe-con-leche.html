Café con Leche / Cuban Coffee with Milk

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Serves 1

The popular café con leche is a fundamental part of every Cuban breakfast. The Cuban version is made with strong concentrated ground Cuban coffee, traditionally brewed in a stovetop moka pot, combined with steamed milk (not scalded milk, as in the Spanish version). It's simple to make and it hits the spot every time.

5 or Fewer Ingredients, Quick

Variation Tip: For a sweet, silky café con leche decrease the sugar by 1 tablespoon and replace it with sweetened condensed milk after the foam has been made in step 3.

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups water
  • 5 tablespoons espresso coffee
  • ¼ cup sugar, plus more as needed
  • 1 cup whole milk

Instructions

  1. Fill a stovetop moka pot according to the manufacturer's instructions using the water and coffee. Brew over medium heat.
  2. Place the sugar in a glass measuring cup or small metal carafe. As soon as the espresso begins to brew, pour the first few drops of coffee (about 1 tablespoon) over the sugar. Continue to brew the coffee.
  3. Meanwhile, use a small spoon to vigorously mix the sugar with the coffee for about 2 minutes until the sugar starts to turn light brown and forms a smooth and creamy texture.
  4. Pour the brewed espresso over the sugar mixture, let the foam rise to the top, and gently stir (rapid stirring will break up the foam layer).
  5. In a small saucepan over medium heat, heat the milk for 4 to 5 minutes until hot and steamy, stirring occasionally to prevent the milk from boiling and developing a film on top. Pour the hot milk into a cup, add the brewed espresso–sugar mixture, and stir to combine. Add sugar and extra espresso to taste. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
