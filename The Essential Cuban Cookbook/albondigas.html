Albondigas / Cuban Meatballs

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Serves 4

If I had to pick my favorite beef main dish in this book, this would be it. I love how tasty these meatballs are, especially when served heaped over fluffy white rice. Leftovers make a great meatball sub.

Technique Tip: Freeze the meatballs for up to 2 months.

Ingredients

For the Meatballs

  • 1½ pounds ground beef (preferably 93% lean)
  • ¼ cup finely diced onion
  • ¼ cup finely diced red bell pepper
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup whole milk
  • 1 cup cracker meal
  • 2 teaspoons yellow mustard
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

For the Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ onion, chopped
  • ½ red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ⅔ cup water
  • ⅓ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

To Make the Meatballs

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, onion, bell pepper, eggs, milk, cracker meal, mustard, salt, and pepper. Using your hands, mix to combine everything, then shape the mixture into about 15 (2-inch) meatballs.
  2. Pour the flour into a shallow bowl, then roll the meatballs in the flour.
  3. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Working in batches, sear the meatballs for 2 to 3 minutes per side until golden brown. Transfer the meatballs to paper towels to drain. Discard the oil and wipe the pot clean.

To Make the Sauce

  1. In the same pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper. Sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until tender. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Pour in the tomato sauce, water, wine, and ketchup and add the sugar, salt, pepper, and meatballs. Partially cover the pot and cook over low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Stir in the parsley and serve warm.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
