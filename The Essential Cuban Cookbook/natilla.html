Natilla / Cinnamon Custard

Serves 6 | Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 4 hours to chill | Cook Time: 30 minutes

Natilla is a sweet egg custard topped with ground cinnamon. It is similar to crema Catalana and crème brûlée. It is very simple to make and requires only staple pantry ingredients, so it almost didn't make the cut to get into this book. Good thing I changed my mind! Every bite is sweet and creamy, with hints of cinnamon and vanilla, plus citrusy notes of lemon.

Variation Tip: For an extra hint of sweetness, use orange peel instead of lemon peel, which will change the color of the natilla slightly but it will be equally delicious.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 (3-inch) strip lemon peel
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 8 large egg yolks
  • 1½ cups granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the milk, lemon peel, cinnamon stick, and salt and cook for about 8 minutes over medium-low heat to bring to a simmer.
  2. In a large bowl, beat the egg yolks and sugar with a handheld electric mixer for about 4 minutes until pale yellow and frothy, or use a wire whisk.
  3. In a small bowl, stir together the cornstarch and water until the cornstarch dissolves, then add the slurry to the egg mixture and beat on low speed until thoroughly mixed.
  4. Slowly beat in half of the hot milk mixture, taking 30 to 40 seconds to do so.
  5. Pour the egg-and-milk mixture back into the saucepan and stir continuously over medium heat, making sure the milk does not stick to the pan. Cook for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring, until thickened. Whisk in the vanilla. Remove from the heat.
  6. Remove and discard the cinnamon stick. Pass the custard through a fine-mesh strainer, and then pour it into one bowl or six individual ramekins. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 4 hours, or until ready to serve. Sprinkle with cinnamon.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
