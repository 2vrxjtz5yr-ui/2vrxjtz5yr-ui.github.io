Classic Daiquiri

Serves 1 | Prep Time: 5 minutes, plus 1 hour to make the syrup

The daiquiri is usually thought of as a frozen fruit drink to be sipped by a pool or at the beach during hot summer months. However, the original classic cocktail, like this recipe, is the complete opposite of a sweet slushie concoction. It is said that the daiquiri originated as a simple sour drink served on the rocks in the small village of Daiquiri, Cuba, in the early 1900s.

Variation Tip: To make it a frozen drink, combine all the ingredients in a blender and blend. To make it a fruity frozen drink, add 1 cup of your favorite fruit and blend.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup crushed ice
  • 2 ounces white rum
  • 1 ounce freshly squeezed lime juice, plus more to taste
  • 1 (¼- to ½-inch) strip lime peel

Instructions

  1. To make a simple syrup, in a small saucepan, combine the sugar and water. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring, until the sugar dissolves. Remove from the heat and let stand at room temperature, covered, for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. In a cocktail shaker or a glass mason jar with a lid, combine the ice, rum, lime juice, and 1 ounce of simple syrup. Cover and shake vigorously for 10 seconds.
  3. Strain into a chilled coupe or serving glass. Garnish with the lime peel.
  4. Refrigerate the remaining simple syrup in an airtight container for up to 3 months.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
