Frijoles Negros / Cuban Black Beans

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 8 hours to soak | Cook Time: 3 hours | 6 servings

Frijoles negros is a classic Cuban side dish. This vegetarian dish not only offers sustenance and comfort but also is loaded with nutrients and is high in both fiber and protein. Traditionally, black beans are eaten with a side of white fluffy rice. Preparing this recipe does take some time and planning, but I promise, you won't be disappointed.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried black beans
  • 1 green bell pepper, quartered
  • 13 cups water, divided
  • ½ cup olive oil, plus 2 tablespoons
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons distilled white vinegar

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the beans and green bell pepper and cover with 5 cups of water. Soak for 8 hours. Drain and rinse, discarding the water.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, combine the soaked beans and green bell pepper and the remaining 8 cups of water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low, partially cover the pot, and simmer for 55 to 60 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove and discard the green pepper.
  3. In a large skillet, heat ½ cup of oil over medium heat. Add the onion and red bell pepper. Sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until tender. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Ladle out 1 cup of black beans into the skillet and smash them with the back of the ladle. Add the smashed bean mixture to the pot with the other beans. Stir in the salt, pepper, cumin, oregano, and bay leaf. Simmer for 45 minutes.
  4. Stir in the sugar and vinegar and simmer for 45 minutes more until thickened. Finish with the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
