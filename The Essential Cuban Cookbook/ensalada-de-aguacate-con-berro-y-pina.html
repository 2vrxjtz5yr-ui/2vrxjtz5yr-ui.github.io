Ensalada de Aguacate con Berro y Piña / Avocado, Pineapple, and Watercress Salad

Prep Time: 10 minutes | 4 servings

I have such fond childhood memories of my grandmother making a wonderful watercress salad. For this version, I combine her salad (watercress is the green of choice in Cuba) with a traditional avocado and pineapple salad. I add my own spin with crispy radishes. The citrus vinaigrette makes this salad shine.

Ingredient Tip: For extra depth of flavor, sear the pineapple chunks in a skillet with 1 tablespoon of butter over medium-high heat.

Technique Tip: The dressing can be made up to 4 days in advance and kept refrigerated.

Variation Tip: Make this a complete meal by adding grilled chicken, shrimp, or steak.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Juice of ½ orange
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 bunches fresh watercress
  • 1 large Florida avocado, or 2 Hass avocados, cubed
  • 2 cups fresh pineapple chunks
  • 2 or 3 radishes, thinly sliced
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk the oil, lime juice, orange juice, garlic, sugar, salt, and pepper to blend. Refrigerate until needed.
  2. Place the watercress in a medium bowl and pour 2 to 3 tablespoons of dressing over it. Toss to coat. Transfer the dressed watercress to a serving platter.
  3. Top with the avocado, pineapple, radishes, and onion. Drizzle with more dressing to taste.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
