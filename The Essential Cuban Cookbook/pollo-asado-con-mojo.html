Pollo Asado con Mojo / Roast Mojo Chicken

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes | Serves 4

Juicy roasted chicken surrounded by a garlicky butter sauce and finished with a splash of fresh lemon juice? Yes, please! Here, I take all the flavorful ingredients found in a mojo sauce and use them separately, without making the actual marinade. The inspiration for this deconstructed mojo came from my obsession with roasted garlic, and it works beautifully in this dish. I really love this recipe and I am pretty sure you will, too.

Variation Tip: For a simpler version, swap the whole chicken for 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs. Cook at 425°F for 35 to 40 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F.

Ingredients

  • 1 (5- to 6-pound) roasting chicken, giblets removed, rinsed and patted dry
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, melted
  • Juice of 1 lemon, divided
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt, divided
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 head garlic, halved horizontally
  • 3 russet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1½ pounds calabaza squash or butternut squash, peeled and sliced
  • 1 onion, thickly sliced

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the melted butter, half the lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of oil, the oregano, mustard, 1½ teaspoons of salt, the paprika, and ¼ teaspoon of pepper.
  3. Place the chicken in a roasting pan and pour the marinade over and inside the chicken. Stuff the cavity with the garlic. Tie the chicken legs together with kitchen string. Scatter the potatoes, squash, and onion around the chicken in the roasting pan. Season the chicken and vegetables with 1½ teaspoons of salt and ¼ teaspoon of pepper and drizzle the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil over the vegetables.
  4. Roast the chicken for 1 to 1½ hours, or until an instant-read thermometer reads 165°F, the meat juices run clear, and the vegetables are fork-tender. Cover and let rest for 10 minutes. Drizzle with the remaining lemon juice and season to taste with salt and pepper.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
