Pastel de Pollo / Chicken Potpie

Prep Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour 15 minutes | Serves 6

Don't be fooled by this all-American title. One bite of this pie and you will be shouting "Viva Cuba!" from the rooftop. Instead of a cream-based filling, the Cuban version has a tomato base, with olives and raisins. This pie is made with store-bought crust, but your favorite homemade crust will work as well.

Technique Tip: To make this ahead, assemble through step 6 and freeze for up to 1 month.

Ingredients

  • 2 (9-inch) unbaked refrigerated piecrusts
  • 3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2½ teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, divided
  • 4 cups water, plus 1 tablespoon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 ounces Spanish chorizo sausage, casing removed, halved lengthwise, then cut into half-moons
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1½ cups tomato sauce
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ¼ cup green pimiento-stuffed olives, diced
  • ¼ cup golden raisins
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 3 large eggs, divided

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Line the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie plate with 1 piecrust. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool.
  3. Meanwhile, season the chicken with 1 teaspoon of salt and ¼ teaspoon of pepper. In a saucepan, combine the seasoned chicken and 4 cups of water and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Cook for 15 minutes. Drain the chicken, reserving 1½ cups of broth. When the chicken is cool enough to handle, use 2 forks to shred the meat. Set aside.
  4. In a large Dutch oven or skillet, heat the oil over medium heat until hot. Add the chorizo, onion, and bell pepper and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 20 to 30 seconds. Pour in the tomato sauce, wine, ½ cup of reserved broth and add the olives, raisins, 1 teaspoon of salt, the cumin, oregano, and shredded chicken. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a blender, combine ½ cup of reserved broth, the milk, 2 eggs, remaining ½ teaspoon of salt, and remaining ¼ teaspoon of pepper. Blend until smooth. Set aside.
  6. Add the filling to the crust and pour in the broth-milk-egg mixture. Place the other piecrust on top of the filling. Trim and crimp the edges and cut 3 slits in the top crust.
  7. In a small bowl, whisk the remaining egg and 1 tablespoon water to blend. Brush the top of the piecrust with the egg wash. Bake for 45 minutes until the crust is golden brown.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
