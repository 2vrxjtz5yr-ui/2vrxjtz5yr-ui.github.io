Pollo a la Plancha / Grilled Chicken Breasts

Prep Time: 15 minutes, plus 30 minutes to marinate | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Serves 4

Pollo a la plancha is on every Cuban family's quick dinner rotation. It is chicken breast flattened until thin, marinated with lime juice and spices, seared on a skillet or griddle until well browned and juicy, and served topped with sautéed onions. I like to serve it with Ensalada de Aguacate con Berro y Piña (page 25). It's simple, healthy, and comes together easily.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Juice of 1 lime, plus more for serving
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large sweet white onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. On a work surface, halve the chicken breasts lengthwise. Lay the chicken pieces between 2 pieces of plastic wrap. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, pound the chicken gently to flatten it to an even ¼-inch thickness. Transfer the chicken to a plate.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together the lime juice, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, pepper, and cumin. Pat each chicken piece dry with a paper towel and pour the lime marinade over the chicken. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. In a large skillet or on a flat griddle, melt the butter and heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until golden brown. Flip and cook the other side for 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer the chicken to a plate and cover with aluminum foil.
  4. In the same skillet, add the onion and garlic and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until soft. Top the chicken with the sautéed onions, garnish with parsley, and serve with additional lime juice if desired.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
