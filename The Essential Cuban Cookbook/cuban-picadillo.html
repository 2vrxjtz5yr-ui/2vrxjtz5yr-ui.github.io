Cuban Picadillo / Braised Ground Beef

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 35 minutes | Serves 4

Picadillo is a quick-cooking saucy ground beef dish with briny olives, sautéed potatoes, spices, and raisins. It is known as Cuban peasants' food. This is the first recipe I learned to make, and it's the one I make the most. My family absolutely loves it, even my son Alec, another one of my picky eaters! Oh, and I always serve it with white rice and a fried egg—delicious!

Variation Tip: For a healthier version, swap ground turkey for the beef.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, washed, peeled, and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green or red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably 93% lean)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup whole green pimiento-stuffed olives
  • ⅓ cup raisins
  • 1 tablespoon capers, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven or skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the potatoes and sauté for 4 to 5 minutes, or until golden. Remove the potatoes from the pot and set aside. Discard all but 2 tablespoons of the oil.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and bell pepper for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 20 to 30 seconds. Mix in the ground beef, bay leaf, tomato sauce, wine, cumin, oregano, salt, sugar, paprika, and pepper. Cook over medium-low heat for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water, if the mixture becomes dry.
  3. Stir in the sautéed potatoes, olives, raisins, and capers. Cook for 15 minutes. Remove from the heat and remove and discard the bay leaf. Garnish with the parsley before serving.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
