Croquetas de Jamon y Chorizo / Ham and Chorizo Croquettes

Prep Time: 15 minutes, plus overnight and 2 hours to chill | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Serves 4 to 6

For the love of croquetas! I cannot begin to tell you how much I love these little snack-size logs filled with creamy béchamel. Croquetas are the star appetizer of any Cuban gathering. Typically served with a wedge of fresh lime and soda crackers on the side, croquetas can be served for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Or all three. Every day.

Worth the Wait

Ingredient Tip: A roux is a combination of flour and fat cooked together and is used to thicken sauces. It is the base of a béchamel sauce.

Technique Tip: Logs can also be shaped by using two spoons or a pastry bag fitted with a large round tip. You can make these croquettes in bulk and freeze them after breading in step 5 for up to 1 month.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons (½ stick) butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1¼ cups whole milk, plus more as needed
  • ⅛ teaspoon grated nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1½ cups grated hickory-smoked ham
  • ¼ cup grated Spanish chorizo sausage
  • 1 tablespoon grated onion
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 cup cracker meal
  • 2 cups oil

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium-low heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1 to 2 minutes. Slowly whisk in the milk, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes, whisking, until thickened to a sauce-like consistency (a roux).
  2. Mix in the ham, chorizo, and onion. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring, until the mixture thickens and separates from the inside walls of the pan. If the mixture is too dry, add a splash of milk.
  3. Transfer the mixture to an 8-by-8-inch baking sheet and spread it into an even layer. Let the mixture cool at room temperature, then cover the baking sheet and refrigerate overnight.
  4. When ready to cook, remove the filling from the refrigerator. Place 2 tablespoons of filling into the palm of your hand and form a log shape. With your other hand, shape each log into a croquette about ¾ inch thick and 3 inches long.
  5. In a shallow bowl, whisk the eggs and water to combine and set aside. Pour the cracker meal into a separate bowl. Dip a croquette into the egg wash and then coat it in cracker meal. Place on a plate and cover. Repeat with the rest of the croquettes. Return the croquettes to the refrigerator and chill for 2 to 4 hours for extra firmness.
  6. Line a plate with paper towels and set aside.
  7. In a large pan, heat enough oil to cover the croquettes completely. Working in batches, fry the croquettes for 3 to 4 minutes until golden brown. Transfer the croquettes to the prepared plate to dry briefly. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
