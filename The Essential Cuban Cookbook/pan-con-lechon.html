Pan con Lechon / Roast Pork Sandwich

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Makes 4 sandwiches

Yes! It is a Cuban tradition to make this sandwich the day after you make Lechon Asado (page 82). Pan con lechon is the Cuban version of a pulled pork sandwich. The pork is piled on top of Cuban bread with raw or sautéed onions and a drizzle of mojo marinade. Serve with ice-cold beer and enjoy.

Quick

Ingredient Tip: If your budget can stretch to it and it is available locally, I recommend buying organic pork. It may be a little more costly, but good-quality pork makes all the difference in this dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large loaf Cuban or French bread, halved lengthwise and cut into 4 equal pieces
  • 3 heaping cups Lechon Asado (page 82), warmed
  • 2 tablespoons mojo marinade (see page 30)
  • 1 tablespoon butter, at room temperature

Instructions

  1. In a small skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and sauté lightly for 3 to 4 minutes until translucent. Remove from the heat.
  2. Generously top half of the bread slices with the hot shredded pork, the mojo marinade, and sautéed onion, all evenly divided. Close the sandwiches with the remaining bread slices and spread the top of each sandwich with the butter.
  3. Preheat a sandwich press or panini grill over medium-high heat. Press the sandwich as flat as possible for 2 to 3 minutes, or until the bread is crispy and light golden brown on the outside. Slice each sandwich on the diagonal and serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
