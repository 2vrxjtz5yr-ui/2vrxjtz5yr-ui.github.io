Fricasé de Pollo / Chicken Fricassee

Prep Time: 15 minutes, plus 30 minutes to marinate | Cook Time: 1 hour | Serves 4

My girls were the pickiest eaters on the planet and thought everything I made was weird. When I finally found a dish they liked, you better believe it went on regular rotation! Chicken fricassee, or as Jessica called it, "that Cuban chicken with olives," was one of their favorites. It's simple to make and kid approved.

Variation Tip: You can use a slow cooker by browning the chicken pieces first, then placing it in the slow cooker and adding all the vegetables and wine. Cover and cook on low heat for about 2½ hours.

Ingredients

  • 1 (3-pound) chicken, quartered and skinned (backbone discarded or kept for stock)
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 8 small new potatoes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ cup pimiento-stuffed Spanish olives
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ teaspoon Tabasco sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken, 1 tablespoon of oil, 1 teaspoon of salt, garlic powder, oregano, and pepper and mix to coat. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken for 3 to 4 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 20 to 30 seconds. Stir in the wine to loosen the browned bits on the bottom of the pot.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot, pour in the tomato sauce and broth, and add the potatoes, bay leaf, remaining 1 teaspoon of salt, and cumin. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat to low, and partially cover the pot. Simmer, stirring occasionally, for 40 to 45 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  5. Stir in the olives, parsley, and Tabasco sauce (if using) and serve warm.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
