Sandwich Cubano / Grilled Cuban Sandwich

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Makes 4 sandwiches

There is a debate between Cuban residents of Miami and Tampa, each claiming authenticity over this classic sandwich. The debate is known as the Cuban Sandwich Crisis. The version here is the one I grew up eating. In Tampa, sliced Genoa salami is added, and sometimes even a little mayo.

Quick

Technique Tip: Assembling a Cuban sandwich has an order to be followed! Working from the bread up: mustard, dill pickles, ham, pork, and Swiss cheese.

Ingredients

  • Yellow mustard, for dressing the bread
  • 1 large loaf Cuban or French bread, halved lengthwise and cut into 4 equal pieces
  • 12 dill pickle slices
  • 1 pound sliced Virginia baked ham, divided
  • 12 ounces shredded or sliced roast pork (see Lechon Asado, page 82)
  • 8 ounces sliced Swiss cheese, divided
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons butter, at room temperature

Instructions

  1. Generously spread the mustard on one side of each bread slice.
  2. Place 3 dill pickle slices on each of 4 bread slices. Then pile a quarter of the ham on top, followed by a quarter of the pork and a quarter of the cheese. Top with the remaining bread slices (mustard-side down) and spread the butter on the outer sides of each sandwich.
  3. Preheat a sandwich press or panini grill over medium-high heat. Press the sandwich as flat as possible for 2 to 3 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the bread is crispy and light golden brown.
  4. Slice each sandwich on the diagonal and serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
