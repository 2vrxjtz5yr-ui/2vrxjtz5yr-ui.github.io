Pudin de Pan con Guayaba / Guava Bread Pudding

Serves 8 | Prep Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes

This recipe comes from a family friend, Caro. When I mentioned I was thinking of dessert ideas for this book, she immediately shared her recipe for guava bread pudding. Naturally, I ran home and tried it. It is the perfect dessert for all the guava lovers out there.

Ingredient Tip: For extra richness, spread 6 tablespoons of room-temperature cream cheese over the custard, before adding the guava marmalade in step 4.

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, 1 tablespoon, at room temperature and 7 tablespoons, melted
  • 2 (1-pound) loaves sliced raisin bread with cinnamon swirl, or 1 (1-pound) loaf brioche bread, cut into cubes
  • 5 large eggs, whisked
  • 1 (15-ounce) can coconut cream
  • 1 (14-ounce) can condensed milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 (16-ounce) can guava marmalade
  • Vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Coat a 10-by-14-inch baking dish with 1 tablespoon of room-temperature butter.
  2. Pour the bread cubes into the prepared pan.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, coconut cream, condensed milk, sugar, cinnamon, vanilla, and salt until smooth. Pour the custard mixture over the bread and stir well. Stir in the 7 tablespoons of melted butter. Set aside for 15 minutes.
  4. Spread the guava marmalade on top of the bread pudding and use a fork to press lightly to help the bread absorb the marmalade, but do not stir.
  5. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes until firm when pressed in the center. Serve warm and topped with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or whipped cream (if using).

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
