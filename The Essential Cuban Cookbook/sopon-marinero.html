Sopón Marinero / Seafood Soup

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | 4 servings

My mom would make this hearty seafood soup in wintertime. It has a fresh tomato base with a touch of saffron and is loaded with seafood. The key to success with this recipe is to buy the freshest fish available and make your own stock using the head and the tail. To make things easier, have your fishmonger clean and cut the fish for you.

Ingredient Tip: To make this soup extra meaty, add 6 to 8 scallops with the fish and shrimp in step 4.

Ingredients

For the Seafood Stock

  • 4 cups cold water
  • 3 to 4 pounds fish heads and bones
  • 8 ounces large shrimp heads and shells
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads

For the Soup

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, coarsely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 dozen littleneck clams, scrubbed
  • 1 dozen mussels, scrubbed
  • 2 pounds fish fillets, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, for serving
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions

To Make the Seafood Stock

  1. In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, combine the water, fish heads and bones, shrimp heads and shells, carrots, celery, onion, garlic, salt, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Remove and discard the fish heads. Place a mesh strainer over a large bowl and carefully strain the stock, discarding all the solids. Add the saffron threads to the hot liquid and let dissolve for 10 minutes.

To Make the Soup

  1. In the same pot used for the stock, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion, tomatoes, and garlic and simmer for 5 minutes. Pour 6 to 8 cups of seafood stock into the pot, along with the wine and paprika.
  2. Add the clams and mussels and simmer for about 5 minutes until they begin to open. Add the fish fillets and shrimp and simmer for 5 minutes. Remove the pot from the heat and discard any clams or mussels that did not open.
  3. Garnish the soup with fresh cilantro and serve with the lime wedges for squeezing.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
