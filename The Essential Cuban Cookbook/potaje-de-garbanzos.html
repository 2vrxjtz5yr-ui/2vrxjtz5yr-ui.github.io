Potaje de Garbanzos / Stewed Chickpeas

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus 8 hours to soak | Cook Time: 2 hours | 6 servings

Potaje de garbanzos is a dish with its roots in Spain. It is a thick chickpea soup perfect for chilly days. The soup can be eaten like a side, with Cuban bread for dipping, or it can be served as a main dish with white rice.

Variation Tip: For a quick meal, use two (15-ounce) cans of organic chickpeas instead of the dried. Skip step 1 and add the potatoes, wine, salt, and pepper as part of step 2. This cuts the total cooking time to about 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried chickpeas
  • 8 cups water, divided, plus more as needed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound smoked ham, cut into 1½-inch cubes
  • 8 ounces Spanish chorizo sausage, casing removed, halved lengthwise, then cut into half-moons
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (8-ounce) ham hock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 red potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons dry sherry or dry white wine
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons hot sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, cover the chickpeas with 5 cups of water. Soak for 8 hours. Drain and rinse, discarding the water.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the ham and chorizo and cook for 3 minutes. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 4 to 5 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Stir in the chickpeas, tomato sauce, ham hock, bay leaf, oregano, cumin, paprika, and the remaining 3 cups of water (or enough to cover all the ingredients by 2 inches). Reduce the heat to medium-low, partially cover the pot, and simmer for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the potatoes, sherry, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of pepper and cook, uncovered, for 30 to 40 minutes until the chickpeas and potatoes are tender.
  4. Remove and discard the ham hock and bay leaf. Season to taste with salt and pepper and garnish with the parsley and hot sauce (if using).

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
