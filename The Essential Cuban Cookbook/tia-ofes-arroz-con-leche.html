Tia Ofe's Arroz con Leche / Rice Pudding

Serves 4 | Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour 10 minutes, plus time to chill

In Cuban culture, when you refer to someone as your Cuban aunt, it usually means that person is a non-relative who is part of your everyday life but not your actual aunt. This recipe comes from our dear Cuban aunt, Tia Ofe. She will occasionally send over some of her creamy rice pudding, and when she does, we fight over it to the last bite.

Variation Tip: For an adult version, soak ½ cup of raisins in ½ cup of white or dark rum and use as a boozy garnish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • ½ cup arroz Valencia or pearl rice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 (3-inch) strip lime peel
  • 1 (12-ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1 (14-ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1½ cups heavy (whipping) cream
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the water, rice, cinnamon stick, and lime peel. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pan, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes until the rice is tender. Remove and discard the lime peel.
  2. Stir in the evaporated milk, condensed milk, heavy cream, salt, and vanilla. Cook, uncovered, over low heat for about 45 minutes until thickened, stirring occasionally. Remove from the heat. Remove and discard the cinnamon stick.
  3. Pour the rice pudding into one bowl or four individual dishes and sprinkle with cinnamon. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve, chilled or at room temperature.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
