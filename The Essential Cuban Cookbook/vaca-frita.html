Vaca Frita / Crispy Beef

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 3 hours | Serves 6

The name of this recipe, which translates literally to "fried cow," may not make the dish sound particularly appetizing, but I assure you it is. And although it takes a while (mostly hands off) to cook, vaca frita is full of flavor and totally worth making. Be sure to cook the beef in batches to avoid crowding the pan.

Variation Tip: Cook some vegetables in the reserved broth until fork-tender in step 4. Add vermicelli noodles, serve with fresh lime wedges, and voilà, you have a Cuban-style beef broth soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds flank steak or skirt steak
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 3 onions, sliced, divided
  • 1 green bell pepper, quartered lengthwise
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced, divided
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 to 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. Season the beef with the salt and pepper.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef and sear for 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and add half the onions, the bell pepper, half the garlic, the bay leaves, and enough water to surround the beef but not completely submerge it. Place the lid on the pot and simmer for 2½ hours, or until the beef is fork-tender, flipping the beef halfway through the cooking time and adding more water, if needed.
  4. Strain the beef, reserving the broth. Discard the onion, bell pepper, garlic, and bay leaves. Transfer the beef to a large plate or platter and let cool. Using two forks, shred the cooled meat into thin strands. Season with the oregano, garlic powder, and cumin.
  5. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the remaining onions and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes. Add the remaining garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Transfer to a plate.
  6. In the same skillet, heat another 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Working in batches to avoid steaming the beef, place half the beef in the skillet in a single layer and cook for 4 to 5 minutes, without stirring, until the bottom is brown and crispy. Using a spatula, turn the beef and cook for 2 to 3 minutes on the other side. Transfer the beef to a plate. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil in the skillet and sear the remaining beef as you did the first half. Transfer to the plate with the other beef. Add the onion and garlic, squeeze fresh lime juice on top, and sprinkle with the cilantro.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
