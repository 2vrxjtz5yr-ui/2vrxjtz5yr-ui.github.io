Tostadas Cubanas / Cuban Toast

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Serves 4

Tostadas, literally meaning "toasted," are a typical breakfast. Technically, though, these tostadas are grilled or pressed instead of toasted. They are made with Cuban bread, a long loaf with a flaky crust and a crumb full of air pockets. A piece is cut from the loaf, then buttered and pressed flat until golden brown.

Ingredient Tip: Two- to three-day-old bread works great for this recipe because it browns better and results in a superior texture.

Technique Tip: A skillet with a cast-iron grill press can be used if you do not have a sandwich press or panini grill.

Variation Tip: Cuban bread has similar characteristics to French bread and Italian bread. Although not traditional, either can be used.

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons butter (Â½ stick plus 1 tablespoon), at room temperature
  • 1 large loaf Cuban bread, halved lengthwise and cut into 4 equal pieces

Instructions

  1. Butter both sides of each piece of bread.
  2. Preheat a sandwich press or panini grill over medium-high heat. Press the bread as flat as possible for 2 to 3 minutes, or until the bread is crispy and light golden brown.
  3. Slice each toast diagonally or in half lengthwise. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
