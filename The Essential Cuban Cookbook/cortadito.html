Cortadito / Cuban Coffee with Evaporated Milk

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Serves 1

A cortadito is a Cuban espresso combined with steamed milk. You might be thinking this sounds exactly like a café con leche, but a cortadito contains less milk and is made with evaporated milk instead of whole milk. Basically, cortadito is a cafecito with a splash of evaporated milk, or as I refer to it—a cup full of deliciousness.

5 or Fewer Ingredients, Quick

Variation Tip: Use an electric espresso maker instead of a moka pot. For a decadent twist, add a drizzle of dulce de leche (caramel) or sweetened condensed milk.

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups water
  • 5 tablespoons espresso coffee
  • ¼ cup sugar, plus more as needed
  • ¼ cup evaporated milk

Instructions

  1. Fill a stovetop moka pot according to the manufacturer's instructions using the water and coffee. Brew over medium heat.
  2. Place the sugar in a glass measuring cup or small metal carafe. As soon as the espresso begins to brew, pour the first few drops of coffee (about 1 tablespoon) over the sugar. Continue to brew the coffee.
  3. Meanwhile, use a small spoon to vigorously mix the sugar with the coffee for about 2 minutes until the sugar starts to turn light brown and forms a smooth and creamy texture.
  4. Pour the brewed espresso over the sugar mixture, let the foam rise to the top, and gently stir (rapid stirring will break up the foam layer).
  5. In a small saucepan over medium heat, heat the milk until hot and steamy, stirring occasionally to prevent the milk from boiling and developing a film on top. Pour the hot milk into a demitasse cup, add the brewed espresso–sugar mixture, and stir to combine. Add sugar and extra espresso to taste. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
