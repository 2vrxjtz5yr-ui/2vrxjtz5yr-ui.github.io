Ropa Vieja / Braised Shredded Beef

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 3 hours | Serves 6

The Cuban legend behind this dish is that there was a poor old man who shredded his clothes because he could not afford to feed his hungry family. The old man started to pray, and a miracle occurred: his old clothes turned into tasty braised shredded beef. Don't you just love happy endings?

Culture Tip: Ropa vieja is the national dish of Cuba and dates back to the Middle Ages of Spanish Sephardi. It is believed to have been brought to Cuba by immigrants from the Canary Islands and was first reported to have been cooked in Cuba in 1857. It is so popular that it is hard not to find it on the menu of any Cuban restaurant.

Variation Tip: Reserve some of the shredded beef before adding it to the sauce to use in Vaca Frita (page 78).

Ingredients

For the Braised Beef

  • 2 pounds chuck roast, flank steak, or skirt steak
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 green bell pepper, quartered lengthwise
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 to 4 cups water

For the Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 (8-ounce) cans tomato sauce
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup pimiento-stuffed Spanish olives, halved crosswise
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ teaspoon Tabasco sauce

Instructions

To Make the Braised Beef

  1. Season the beef on both sides with the salt and pepper.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Sear the beef for 3 to 4 minutes per side until golden brown.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, add the onion, bell pepper, garlic, bay leaves, and enough water to surround the beef but not completely submerge it. Cover the pot and simmer for 2½ hours, or longer, until the beef is fork-tender, flipping the beef halfway through the cooking time and adding more water, if needed.
  4. Strain the beef, reserving 1 cup of broth. Discard the vegetables and bay leaves. Once the beef is cool enough to handle, use two forks to shred the meat into thin strands. Set aside.

To Make the Sauce

  1. In the same pot used to cook the beef, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until tender. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds.
  2. Return the shredded beef to the pot.
  3. Pour in the tomato sauce, white wine, and reserved broth and add the oregano, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Lower the heat to low and cook, covered, for 15 or 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in the olives, parsley, and Tabasco.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
