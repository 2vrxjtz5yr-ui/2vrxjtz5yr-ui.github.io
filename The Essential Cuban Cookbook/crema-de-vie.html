Crema de Vie / Cuban Eggnog

Serves 12 | Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes, plus 15 minutes to cool

This Cuban-style eggnog is a traditional holiday favorite, generally enjoyed during Christmas and New Year's Eve. It is commonly given to friends and neighbors as gifts in a decorative bottle. You can make it ahead and keep refrigerated for up to a month—the longer it sits, the better it tastes. Don't forget to keep a bottle for yourself!

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 (12-ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1 (14-ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup white or gold rum
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions

  1. To make a simple syrup, in a small saucepan, combine the sugar, water, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring, until the sugar dissolves. Remove from the heat and let stand at room temperature to cool. Remove the cinnamon stick from the syrup and transfer it to a serving bottle.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks.
  3. In a small saucepan, bring the evaporated milk to a boil over medium-low heat. Once it reaches 190°F, remove the pan from the heat. Slowly pour the hot milk, a few drops at a time, into the egg yolks, whisking continuously until combined. Pour the evaporated milk–egg mixture back into the saucepan and simmer for 4 minutes over medium-low heat. Remove from the heat and let cool for 15 minutes.
  4. In a blender, combine the simple syrup, condensed milk, rum, vanilla, salt, and the warm evaporated milk–egg mixture. Blend for 30 seconds. Transfer to the serving bottle with the cinnamon stick.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
