Camarones al Ajillo / Garlic Shrimp

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | 4 servings

Unlike most other Cuban comfort foods, there is no red sauce involved in this recipe. This simple dish might look like just shrimp and garlic, but the paprika, sherry, and lemon juice elevate the flavor to something special. The only thing that could make this dish better would be crusty bread for a delicious sauce dipper.

Technique Tip: Be careful not to burn the garlic or it will make the sauce bitter.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp (21 to 25 count), peeled, deveined, and patted dry
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon smoked Spanish paprika
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 8 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons dry sherry
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, season the shrimp with the salt and paprika. Toss to combine.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the oil over medium-low heat. Add the garlic and cook for about 2 minutes until it starts to turn light golden, making sure the garlic does not burn.
  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the shrimp. Sear for about 2 minutes. Pour in the sherry and cook for about 1 minute, stirring continuously, until the shrimp are cooked through. Remove from the heat.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and parsley. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
