Macarrones a lo Cubano / Cuban Pasta

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 50 minutes | 6 servings

Chorizo, ham, and a savory sofrito loaded with garlic—this is what my abuelita referred to as macarrones a lo Cubano. My grandmother was an amazing cook and had a knack for creating comfort foods, and she nailed it with this one. This dish might have been influenced by the Italians, but the Cuban seasoning really shines.

Ingredient Tip: For extra indulgence, add room-temperature burrata right before serving. It makes a wonderful addition.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 12 ounces hickory-smoked ham or serrano ham, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 8 ounces Spanish chorizo sausage, casing removed, halved lengthwise, then cut into half-moons
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1½ cups heavy (whipping) cream
  • 3 tablespoons dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil leaves, roughly torn
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt, plus 1 teaspoon
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pound dried rigatoni pasta or any short pasta
  • 2 cups coarsely grated fontina cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the ham and chorizo and cook for 3 minutes until some of the fat renders from both meats. Add the onion and bell pepper and reduce the heat to medium. Sauté for 4 to 5 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Stir in the tomatoes, heavy cream, wine, basil, oregano, paprika, 1 teaspoon of salt, and the pepper. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  3. Season a large pot of water with the remaining 2 tablespoons of salt and bring to a boil. Cook the pasta for 7 minutes, or until al dente (it will continue to cook while baking). Drain the pasta, reserving 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, and add the pasta to the pan with the sauce.
  4. Add ½ cup of pasta water and half the fontina cheese and toss to coat evenly, adding a little more pasta water, if needed, to thin the sauce. Transfer the pasta into a large baking dish and sprinkle with the remaining fontina.
  5. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the cheese melts and is lightly browned on top.
  6. Right before serving, drizzle the pasta with oil and sprinkle with the fresh oregano.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
