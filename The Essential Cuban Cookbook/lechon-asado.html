Lechon Asado / Roast Pork

Prep Time: 15 minutes, plus 24 hours to marinate | Cook Time: 7 hours 15 minutes, plus 30 minutes to rest | Serves 8

After seven hours and more of waiting, waiting, and waiting you will be thrilled to have this garlicky lechon asado in your kitchen—trust me, it's worth waiting for. This pork dish is perfect to serve to a crowd, in a Cuban sandwich or pan con lechon. To turn it into Cuban tacos, I grab corn tortillas, pile them high with the pork, and top with onions and avocado slices.

Technique Tip: Use a food processor or a high-powered blender instead of a mortar and pestle.

Ingredients

  • 20 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 3 tablespoons kosher salt, divided
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 (8- to 12-pound) bone-in, skin-on pork shoulder

Instructions

  1. To make the marinade, using a mortar and pestle, combine the garlic, 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt, and the pepper. Smash to form a paste. Add the lime juice, orange juice, 1 tablespoon of salt, the oregano, and cumin and stir to combine.
  2. Pierce the pork as many times as you can with a sharp knife. Rub 1 tablespoon of salt all over the pork and place it in a large bowl. Pour the marinade over the pork, cover, and refrigerate for 24 hours.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  4. Remove the marinated pork from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature. Pat dry with a paper towel. Sprinkle the remaining 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt all over the dried skin. Place the pork, skin-side up, in a roasting pan.
  5. Roast for 1 hour. Reduce the heat to 275°F, cover the pork with aluminum foil, and cook for 6 hours, or until the meat can be shredded easily with a fork.
  6. Change the oven setting to broil.
  7. Remove the foil and broil the pork for 15 minutes until the skin is crispy and golden brown. Remove the pan from the oven, tent with foil, and let rest for 25 to 30 minutes. Shred the pork with two forks and serve on a platter.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
