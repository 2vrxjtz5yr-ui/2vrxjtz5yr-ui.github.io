Tamal en Hoja / Fresh Corn Tamales

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 2 hours 30 minutes | Serves 6

Tamales in Cuba can be traced back to the Ciboney, the Indigenous People of the island. Tamales are popular throughout Latin America and the Caribbean. Although this dish is usually favored during the holidays, Cubans enjoy it any time of year. The addition of hot sauce and fresh lime juice elevates the flavor.

Worth the Wait

Technique Tip: Uncooked tamales can be frozen after they are wrapped for up to 6 months.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup olive oil, plus 2 tablespoons
  • 1½ pounds pork, cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ½ red bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ green bell pepper, chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cups fresh corn kernels (from about 4 ears)
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, at room temperature
  • ½ to 1 cup finely ground yellow cornmeal
  • 2 (8-ounce) packs corn husks, or 12 dried, soaked in warm water for 30 minutes, drained

Instructions

  1. Line a plate with paper towels and set aside.
  2. In a Dutch oven or large skillet, heat ¼ cup of oil over medium heat. Add the pork and fry for 8 to 10 minutes, or until golden. Transfer to the prepared plate. Discard the rendered fat.
  3. In the same pot, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat. Add the onion and red and green bell peppers and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for 20 to 30 seconds. Stir in the tomato sauce, wine, oregano, cumin, salt, pepper, and pork. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. In a food processor or a high-powered blender, combine the corn kernels and butter and pulse until smooth but with some corn kernels still visible. Transfer the corn puree to the pan with the sauce and pork and stir to combine.
  5. Gradually stir in ½ cup of cornmeal and continue to cook over low heat, stirring frequently, for 12 to 15 minutes, or until thickened. Add water or more cornmeal, if needed, to achieve a manageable consistency. Taste and adjust the seasoning, if needed.
  6. Place 2 corn husks flat on a clean surface, overlapping. Spread 2½ tablespoons of corn filling in the center of the husks, leaving a 1-inch border on each side. Bring the long sides of the corn husks together. Fold both ends of the corn husks in toward the center and over the filling. Tie with string to secure the ends.
  7. Pour ½ inch of water into a large pot. Place a steamer basket inside and arrange the tamales, seam-side down, in the basket. Cover the pot and simmer for 1½ to 2 hours, adding water as needed. Remove the tamales from the steamer and serve.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
