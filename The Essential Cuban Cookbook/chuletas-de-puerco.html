Chuletas de Puerco / Pork Chops

Prep Time: 10 minutes, plus at least 4 hours to brine and 20 minutes for the chops to come to room temperature | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Serves 4

Brining is a method used to tenderize lean cuts of meat. I never knew the term as a kid, but I always saw my grandmother soaking pork chops in a water bath for a few hours before cooking them. A good brine is the secret to juicy chops. Top with salsa verde (see page 46) and enjoy!

Ingredient Tip: When selecting pork chops, look for a pinkish-red color with some marbling in the meat.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt, plus 1 teaspoon
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 9 garlic cloves, slightly crushed, divided
  • 8 black peppercorns
  • 4 oregano sprigs, divided
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups ice cubes
  • 4 bone-in center-cut pork chops
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water, 1/4 cup of salt, the sugar, 8 garlic cloves, the peppercorns, 3 oregano sprigs, and the bay leaf to a boil over high heat. Simmer for about 3 minutes until the salt and sugar dissolve. Pour the brine into a heatproof bowl, add the ice, and let cool. Submerge the pork chops in the cooled brine. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  3. Remove the chops from the brine and pat them dry with paper towels. Let sit at room temperature for 20 minutes. Season with the remaining 1 teaspoon of salt and the ground pepper.
  4. In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the chops and sear for about 5 minutes per side until golden brown. Add the butter, the remaining garlic clove, and the remaining oregano sprig to the skillet and cook until the butter melts. Using a spoon, baste the chops with the butter mixture, then transfer the skillet to the oven.
  5. Roast for 5 to 6 minutes, or until an instant-read thermometer registers 145°F. Rest the meat for 5 minutes. Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
