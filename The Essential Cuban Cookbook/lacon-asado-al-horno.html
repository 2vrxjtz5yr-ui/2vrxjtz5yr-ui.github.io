Lacon Asado al Horno / Oven-Roasted Ham Hocks

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes | Serves 4

Technically, smoked ham hocks do not need additional cooking; however, by cooking them again, they become extra tender on the inside and extra crispy on the outside. This recipe can also be adapted easily to the grill. The ham hocks go great with a simple side of boiled or roasted potatoes and a fresh garden salad.

Technique Tip: Prepare the grill and clean the grill grates for cooking over medium-high heat at 350°F. Place the foil packets on the grill and cook for 1 1/2 hours, turning once halfway through the grilling time.

Ingredient Tip: Keep a cooked packet in the freezer to add to your next pot of beans.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 6 ham hocks, with the skin bone exposed
  • Olive oil cooking spray, or 1 tablespoon oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, oregano, garlic powder, cumin, and pepper. Rub the spice mixture on the ham hocks.
  3. Create 6 aluminum foil packets. To make a foil packet, prepare two (8-inch) sheets of heavy-duty foil for each packet. Layer one sheet of foil on top of the other sheet. Coat with cooking spray and place a ham hock in the center of each packet. Seal each packet by bringing the two edges toward each other and pressing to create a tight seal. Fold the seal over several times toward the food. Seal the other two ends. Put the packets on a baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 1 1/2 hours, turning once halfway through the baking time.
  5. Remove from the heat and let the packets cool before carefully opening the packets to serve.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
