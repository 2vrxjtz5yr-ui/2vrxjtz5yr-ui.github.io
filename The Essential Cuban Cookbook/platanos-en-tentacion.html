Plátanos en Tentación / Caramel-Glazed Sweet Plantains

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | 6 servings

Plátanos en tentación literally translates to "plantains in temptation." Need I say more? But let's applaud the Cubans for their clever name choice and for their daring to take this fruit and roast it with a sticky caramel sauce. This is considered a side dish, but it can totally stand alone as a dessert.

Variation Tip: Replace the white wine with brandy, Madeira, or rum.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons butter (½ stick), divided, at room temperature
  • ¼ cup packed dark brown sugar
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 ripe sweet plantains, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 cinnamon stick, cut into 9 pieces
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Coat the inside of a shallow baking dish evenly with 1 tablespoon of butter.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together the brown sugar and granulated sugar.
  3. Place the whole plantains in the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle half the sugar mixture over the plantains. Pour the wine and maple syrup over the plantains and gently stab each plantain with 3 small pieces of cinnamon stick. Sprinkle with the remaining sugar mixture, the cinnamon, and salt. Dot small pieces of the remaining 3 tablespoons of butter over the plantains.
  4. Bake, uncovered, for 50 to 60 minutes, or until the plantains are fork-tender and golden brown.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
