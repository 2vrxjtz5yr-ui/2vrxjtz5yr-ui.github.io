Flan de Queso / Cream Cheese Flan

Serves 8 | Prep Time: 5 minutes, plus 2 hours to chill | Cook Time: 1 hour, plus 1 hour to cool

Flan remains one of the quintessential Cuban desserts. This is my take on the traditional flan, to which I add cream cheese for an even creamier texture.

Variation Tip: For a traditional flan, omit the cream cheese.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 (14-ounce) can condensed milk
  • 1 (12-ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¾ cup sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Set a 9-inch round cake pan inside a 9-by-13-inch roasting pan.
  2. In a blender, combine the cream cheese, condensed milk, evaporated milk, eggs, vanilla, and salt. Process until smooth. Set aside.
  3. To make the caramel, in a medium saucepan, pour the sugar in an even layer and heat over medium heat for 2 minutes. Do not stir. Gently swirl the pan for 3 to 4 minutes until the sugar starts to turn a pale golden brown, at which point it is okay to start stirring with a wooden spoon. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the caramel is an even medium golden brown. Take care not to burn the caramel. Pour the caramel into the round cake pan. Set aside to let the caramel set.
  4. Pour the custard over the set caramel. Slowly pour hot water into the larger roasting pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the cake pan, making sure the water does not fall into the custard.
  5. Bake for 45 to 50 minutes, or until the custard is set around the edges, with a slight jiggle in the center. Remove from the water and cool at room temperature for 1 hour. Once cool, refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  6. To remove the flan from the pan, run a knife around the outer edge of the flan. Place a large plate on top of the pan and flip it quickly to avoid any caramel spilling. This dessert is best served chilled.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
