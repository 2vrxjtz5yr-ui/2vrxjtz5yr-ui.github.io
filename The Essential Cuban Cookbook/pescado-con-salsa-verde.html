Pescado con Salsa Verde / Baked Fish with Green Salsa

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | 4 servings

One of my strongest memories of my dad is of him making a delicious herbaceous green sauce and slathering it on white fish. I was never able to get the original recipe, but here is my way of recreating that green goodness. And, in my not so humble opinion, this sauce is pretty fabulous!

Ingredient Tip: Buy the freshest fish you can find. Some of my favorites for this recipe are flounder, grouper, halibut, red snapper, and yellowtail.

Ingredients

For the Salsa Verde

  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 2 cups fresh parsley leaves, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons capers, rinsed and drained
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Kosher salt

For the Fish

  • 4 (6-ounce) white fish fillets, patted dry
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup chicken or fish broth
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 2 garlic cloves, thinly sliced

Instructions

To Make the Salsa Verde

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the oil, parsley, garlic, lemon juice, capers, and pepper. Season to taste with salt.

To Make the Fish

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Season the fish with the salt and pepper, then lightly dust with the flour.
  3. In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Carefully place the fish into the hot oil and cook for 3 to 4 minutes on each side until light golden brown. Remove the fish from the skillet.
  4. Pour in the broth and wine, add the garlic to the skillet, and bring to a boil. Return the fish to the skillet and cover it. Transfer to the oven and bake for 10 to 15 minutes, until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Remove from the oven, pour the salsa verde over the fish, and serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
