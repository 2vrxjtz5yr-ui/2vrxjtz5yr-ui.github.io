Cafecito / Cuban Coffee

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Serves 4 to 6

The Cuban coffee culture is so intense that, in most kitchens, the moka pot lives on the stovetop. Coffee is the center of all things social. It's even a trend these days to have an entire Cuban coffee cart or station at weddings and quinceañera celebrations, dedicated to making your favorite coffee drink. My daughter Janice and her husband Carlos even made Cuban coffee-themed gift bags to give to their out-of-town wedding guests.

5 or Fewer Ingredients, Quick

Technique Tip: Be prepared! Have your carafe or measuring glass, sugar, and spoon ready for those first few drops of coffee to brew. This is essential for a thick layer of foam. Cafecito is best when served in demitasse cups, also known as tacitas.

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cups water
  • 5 tablespoons espresso coffee
  • ¼ cup sugar

Instructions

  1. Fill a stovetop moka pot according to the manufacturer's instructions using the water and coffee. Brew over medium heat.
  2. Place the sugar in a glass measuring cup or small metal carafe. As soon as the espresso begins to brew, pour the first few drops of coffee (about 1 tablespoon) over the sugar. Continue to brew the coffee.
  3. Meanwhile, use a small spoon to vigorously mix the sugar with the coffee for about 2 minutes until the sugar starts to turn light brown and forms a smooth and creamy texture.
  4. Pour the brewed espresso over the sugar mixture, let the foam rise to the top, and gently stir (rapid stirring will break up the foam layer). Serve immediately.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
