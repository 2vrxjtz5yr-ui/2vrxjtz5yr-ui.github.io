Masitas de Puerco Fritas / Fried Pork Chunks

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 2 hours, plus 30 minutes to cool | Serves 4

In Cuban cuisine, pork and mojo go together like peanut butter and jelly. The bright, tangy mojo balances the fatty pork well. The combination of the citrus, garlic, and spices brings life to the pork. Some things are just naturally better together.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork loin or shoulder, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 3 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup pasture-raised pork lard, at room temperature
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 3 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, season the pork with 2 teaspoons of salt and toss to coat.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pot, combine the pork, water, and lard and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer, partially covered, for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender and all the water has evaporated. Remove the pork from the heat and let cool for 30 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, make the mojo. Using a mortar and pestle, combine the garlic and remaining 1 teaspoon of salt. Crush for 2 to 3 minutes to form a smooth paste. Stir in the lime juice, orange juice, oregano, and pepper. Set aside.
  4. Lightly brown the pork in the rendered fat in the pot over medium-high heat for about 10 minutes until crispy. Transfer to a platter and cover with aluminum foil.
  5. In the same pot, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and mojo. Saut√© for 3 to 4 minutes and pour the mixture over the pork to serve.
