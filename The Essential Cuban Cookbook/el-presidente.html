El Presidente / The President

Serves 1 | Prep Time: 5 minutes

A spin on the classic cocktail Manhattan, El Presidente is thought to have been created in the early 1920s by Eddie Woelke, an American bartender in Havana. He created the drink for the Cuban president at the time and it immediately became the favorite cocktail of the Cuban upper class. I'm about 100 years late to this party, but I'm hooked.

Variation Tip: An Amarena cherry makes a great garnish.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces aged or golden rum
  • 1 ounce blanc vermouth (not dry vermouth)
  • 1 ounce orange curaçao or Grand Marnier
  • 1 teaspoon grenadine
  • Ice cubes, for chilling
  • 1 (1-inch) strip orange peel

Instructions

  1. In a tall glass, combine the rum, blanc vermouth, orange curaçao, and grenadine.
  2. Fill the glass two-thirds with ice and stir until cold. Strain into a chilled coupe or serving glass.
  3. Squeeze the orange peel into the drink to release the oils, discard the peel, and serve.

Source: Recipe from The Essential Cuban Cookbook
