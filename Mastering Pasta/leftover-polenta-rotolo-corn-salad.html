Leftover-Polenta Rotolo with Corn Salad

4 servings
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Adam Leonti was reading The River Cafe Cook Book and came up with a few ideas for using leftover polenta. Italians use it all the time for pasticciata (baked in a casserole), but they also grill and fry leftover polenta. Adam decided to make a rotolo with it. Since polenta is corn, it made sense to add a nice corn salad on top. If you make a big pot of polenta during the summer when fresh corn is in season, use the leftovers here.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups (480 g) warm polenta (page 247)
  • 1¼ cups (125 g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 4 ounces (114 g) Egg Yolk Dough (page 26), rolled into a sheet about 1⁄16 inch (1.5 mm) thick
  • 3 tablespoons (43 g) unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 tablespoon (30 ml) olive oil
  • 3 ears corn, shucked and kernels cut from cobs
  • 1 tablespoon (15 ml) white truffle paste (see Sources, page 249)
  • 1 tablespoon (4 g) chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 1 teaspoon (5 ml) sherry vinegar

Instructions

  1. Stir together the polenta and 1 cup (100 g) of the Parmesan. Taste the mixture, adding salt and pepper until it tastes good to you. Stir in the egg.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop in the entire pasta sheet and blanch it for 30 seconds, stirring and separating it with tongs to keep the sheet from sticking to itself. Carefully remove the pasta sheet and lay it out on kitchen towels or paper towels. Trim the edges square.
  3. Spread the warm polenta mixture evenly over the entire sheet of pasta, all the way to the edges. Starting from a short side, tightly roll up the sheet. The rotolo should look like a jelly roll. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate until firm, at least 1 hour or up to 4 hours.
  4. Heat the oven to 500°F (260°C). Generously butter a shallow 2-quart (2 L) baking dish, or, for individual servings, butter four 2-cup (473 ml) baking dishes. Using a sharp plain-edged or serrated knife, trims the ends of the rotolo, then cut it crosswise into four slices, each about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick. Lay the slices flat in the baking dish(es) and top each slice with about 2 teaspoons butter and 1 tablespoon of the remaining Parmesan.
  5. Bake the rotolo until heated through and lightly browned on top, 10 to 15 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the corn kernels and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the truffle paste, parsley, and vinegar. Taste the mixture, adding salt and pepper until it tastes good to you.
  7. Transfer the rotolo slices to warmed plates if baked in a single dish, or leave in the individual baking dishes. Top each serving with some of the corn salad.

Source: Recipe from Mastering Pasta: The Art and Practice of Handmade Pasta, Gnocchi, and Risotto by Marc Vetri with David Joachim
