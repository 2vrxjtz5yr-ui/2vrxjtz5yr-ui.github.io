Extruded Semolina Dough

Makes about 1 pound (454 g)

This is a basic semolina-and-water dough used for extruded pasta shapes and hand-formed pasta like orecchiette. The dough is firmer than egg-based pasta doughs and is ideal for shapes that need to hold their structure.

Note: This dough can be used for orecchiette, cavatelli, and other hand-formed pasta shapes. For orecchiette, add extra water to create a dough you can shape by hand.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (335 g) semolina
  • â…” cup (177 ml) water

Instructions

  1. Put the semolina in a bowl and slowly stir in the water. Knead the dough into a ball with your hands. It should be stiff but rollable. Shape the dough into a disk and set it aside, covered, for at least 30 minutes, or wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 3 days.
  2. When ready to use, the dough can be extruded through a pasta machine or used for hand-formed shapes. If using for hand-formed pasta like orecchiette, you may need to add additional water to create a more pliable dough that can be shaped by hand.

Source: Recipe from Mastering Pasta: The Art and Practice of Handmade Pasta, Gnocchi, and Risotto by Marc Vetri with David Joachim (page 120)
