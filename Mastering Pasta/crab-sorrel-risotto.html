Crab and Sorrel Risotto

Servings: 4

Source: Mastering Pasta (Page 232)

Years ago, I made a crab lasagna with sorrel and loved the flavor. Sorrel adds the spark of lemon without any actual lemon juice. It's a cool combination that I decided to revive in a risotto dish.

Ingredients

  • 6 tablespoons (90 ml) olive oil
  • ¾ cup (42 g) finely chopped yellow onion
  • 1 cup (195 g) Carnaroli rice
  • ⅓ cup (78 ml) dry white wine
  • 4 to 5 cups (946 ml to 1.2 L) crab stock (page 243), heated to a boil
  • 8 ounces (227 g) jumbo lump crabmeat, picked over to remove any shells or cartilage
  • 1½ cups (50 g) chopped fresh sorrel, plus some chiffonade for garnish
  • Grated zest of 1 lemon
  • 4 fresh chives, chopped
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons (30 ml) of the oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and sweat it until it is soft but not browned, 4 to 6 minutes. Add the rice and sauté the rice until it is translucent around the edges and starts to brown a little, 2 to 3 minutes. This is an important step to develop the right flavor and texture in the rice; make sure the rice absorbs some of the oil. Pour in the wine and simmer it until the pan goes dry, 2 to 3 minutes.
  2. Gradually add the boiling crab stock about ½ cup (118 ml) at a time, stirring almost constantly over medium-high heat until the rice absorbs the stock after each addition. When the rice is done, it should be tender but still a little chewy when bitten and have released most of its starch, creating a creamy sauce in the pan. This process will take 25 to 30 minutes. Near the end of cooking, add a little more stock if necessary and stir the rice like crazy to release as much starch as possible and create the sauce. Drag your spoon through the rice to the bottom of the pan; the sauce should slowly fill in the line you made.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and gradually add the remaining 4 tablespoons (60 ml) oil, stirring until the risotto is creamy. Stir in the crabmeat, chopped sorrel, lemon zest, and chives. Taste the risotto, adding salt and pepper until it tastes good to you.
  4. Dish out the risotto onto warmed plates and garnish with the sorrel chiffonade.
