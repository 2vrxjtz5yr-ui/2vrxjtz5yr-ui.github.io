3-2-1 Brine

About 3 gallons (11.4 L)

Here's my all-purpose brine for pork, chicken, or almost anything that tends to dry out when cooked. The brine helps keep meat moist and it's easy to remember: 3 gallons (11.4 L) water, 2 pounds (907 g) kosher salt, 1 pound (454 g) sugar. I included volume measurements here in case you don't have a scale to measure by weight. This recipe makes enough to brine about 20 pounds (9 kg) of meat. Halve or quarter the recipe as needed.

Ingredients

  • 3 gallons (11.4 L) water
  • 2 pounds (907 g) kosher salt (about 3 cups)
  • 1 pound (454 g) sugar (about 2Â¼ cups)
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 10 sprigs rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon (6 g) fennel seeds
  • 15 black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf, crumbled

Instructions

  1. Pour the water into a large container, add the salt and sugar, and stir until dissolved. Transfer 2 cups (473 ml) of the brine to a food processor and add the garlic, rosemary, fennel seeds, peppercorns, and bay leaf. Puree until all of ingredients are finely chopped. Return the pureed mixture to the brine and stir well.
  2. To use the brine, submerge the meat in it and refrigerate it as directed in the recipe you are using. In general, I like to brine meat for about 1 day per pound of bone-in meat.

Source: Recipe from Mastering Pasta: The Art and Practice of Handmade Pasta, Gnocchi, and Risotto by Marc Vetri with David Joachim
