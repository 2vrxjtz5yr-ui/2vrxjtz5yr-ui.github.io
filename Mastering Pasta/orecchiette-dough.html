Orecchiette Dough

Yield: About 1 pound (454 g) Source: Mastering Pasta (p.176) Type: Base Recipe

For orecchiette, I use a semolina-and-water dough similar to Extruded Pasta Dough (page 120). Instead of extruding it, I add extra water to create a dough you can shape by hand. Try this shape in Orecchiette with Pig's Head Ragù and Black Walnuts (page 194).

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (335 g) semolina
  • ¾ cup (177 ml) water

Instructions

  1. Mix the Dough: Put the semolina in a bowl and slowly stir in the water. Knead the dough into a ball with your hands. It should be stiff but rollable.
  2. Rest the Dough: Shape the dough into a disk and set it aside, covered, for least 30 minutes, or wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 3 days.
